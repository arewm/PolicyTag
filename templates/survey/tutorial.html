Google has developed Photos service as a way to automatically back up and sync photos and videos between all of your devices. When you upload media to Photos, is automatically processed and some tags are assigned using a classification algorithm.

For the purpose of this survey, imagine that Google has developed a new feature for Photos that allows you to automatically sync media to specific destinations according to user-defined policies. This system leverages the automatically generated tags to define these sharing policies, control media export on a personal basis.

The tags are divided into 5 general categories:

Photo type
Generic description of the media (i.e. photo, video). This category also includes the labels describing content generated by Google's auto-editing functionality.
Location
Where the photo was taken. The location is described relative to Work and Home, two places that you can define for your Google profile
Things
Objects that Google has determined exist in your photos
Photo properties
Information associated with the by the camera that took the picture
Temporal
Describes the time that the photo was taken by type of day, date of creation, and time of creation

Each of these tag categories also allows you to specify a custom tag to filter on. In order to create a policy, simply drag the relevant tags to the workspace, specify whether each action should be allowed or denied, and click the green check mark.

All completed policies will show up in the bottom of the page.
